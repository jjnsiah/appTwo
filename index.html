ADMIN BLOCK

The admission block is the location of the office of the president and other prestitious personnel in the school. As the name suggests, it has the offices of all officials in charge of admission. Additionally, the Business Administration department offices are also found on this same block.

LIBRARY

Ashesi's library is primarily digital, with subscriptions to a number of electronic information resources, including the PERI databases which give access to over 30,000 scholarly journals. Ashesi's library is not limited to the library building; it reaches out, with cutting edge information, into faculty and administrative offices, classrooms, cafeterias, and even outdoor gathering spaces. The electronic library is supplemented by a paper collection that is currently 24,000 volumes, as well as a variety of international and local magazines, newspapers, and research publications

LABS

For computer science and engineering majors, this block is home. This is where they have their course lab and lecture sessions. For the best internet access in the school, that is the right place to be. The labs are also equipped with top notch technological equipment to assist students to study.

ENGINEERING

This block host Ashesi’s new engineering majors – Computer Engineering, Electrical and Electronic Engineering, and Mechanical Engineering – which places emphasis on hands-on learning, entrepreneurship, design and problem solving,to equip students with skills to create long-term solutions to Africa’s engineering challenges and deploy better-designed infrastructure for local needs. This block also hosts the lecturers in the engineering department.

HOSTELS

To provide positive living experiences, Ashesi has twenty-two apartments, spread within two main residence halls, that houses approximately 400 students. The apartments have options for five,four and two people in a room, which are assigned on a lottery basis to avoid bias. To help make residential life easier, each hall has Residence Assistants (RAs) - student leaders selected by the Student Affairs Office - to provide extended support to students. RAs work to promote a friendly atmosphere of social interaction and create an environment that is conducive to academic study.
